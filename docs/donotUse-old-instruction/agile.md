Development Guideline: Collaboration & Communication
Overview

Successful projects depend on effective communication and teamwork. This guideline outlines how to collaborate within the dataFootV1 team and interact with stakeholders.

Agile Practices

Participate fully in Agile ceremonies: sprint planning, daily stand‑ups, sprint reviews and retrospectives. These meetings ensure alignment, transparency and continuous improvement.

Estimate tasks realistically based on complexity and available capacity. Identify dependencies and potential blockers early.

Cross‑Team Collaboration

Communicate dependencies and changes across front‑end, back‑end, database, machine‑learning and design teams. Use shared documents, diagrams and regular syncs to maintain alignment.

Involve relevant roles early in planning. For example, consult the database architect when designing new endpoints or the design expert when planning UI changes.

Issue Tracking & Documentation

Use the project’s issue tracking tool to create, assign and update tasks. Provide clear descriptions, acceptance criteria and links to relevant user stories or documentation.

Keep project documentation and guidelines discoverable and up to date. Encourage others to contribute improvements and corrections.

Respectful Communication

Foster a culture of respect and inclusivity. Listen to diverse perspectives and encourage constructive discussion.

Provide feedback thoughtfully and receive feedback with openness. Address conflicts professionally and seek resolution through dialogue.
